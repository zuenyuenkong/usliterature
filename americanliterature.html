  • 二：主义综述和详细考点
    • Puritanism (1620-1820) 清教主义
      • Captain John Smith, William Bradford, Martin Luther, John Calvin, John Cotton, Roger Williams, Anne Bradstreet, Michael Wigglesworth, Edward Taylor, Jonathan Edwards， Nathaniel Hawthorne* (Puritanism, Romanticism, Transcendentalism)
      • 时期综述
        • To purify the Church of England and restored it to the "purity" of the first-century church as established by Jesus Christ himself condition. Bible as guide to all aspects of life. They made it their chief business to see that man lived and thought and acted in a way which tended to the glory of God. 净化英国教会，将其恢复到耶稣基督亲自建立的第一世纪教会的 "纯洁 "状态。以圣经作为生活各方面的指导。他们的主要任务是确保人的生活、思想和行为都倾向于上帝的荣耀。
        • Basic Theological Doctrines
          1. total depravity, that because of the original sin of Adam and Eve, all mankind was born corrupt and prone to evil. 由于亚当和夏娃的原罪，所有人类都生来堕落，容易作恶。
          1. Jesus' sacrifice had earned God's forgiveness, or grace, only for the elected 耶稣的牺牲赢得了上帝的宽恕，或者说是恩典只为被选中的人。
          1. salvation was ordained by God, and one could neither win nor lose salvation by his own act. 因信称义:救赎是由上帝规定的，一个人既不能靠自己的行为赢得也不能失去救赎。
          1. predestination: God had predestined all event and selected all those who would be saved 命运决定论: 宿命。上帝已经预设了所有的事件，并选择了所有将被拯救的人
          1. religious teaching tended to emphasize the image of a wrathful God. 强调上帝严厉的一面: 宗教教义倾向于强调愤怒的神的形象
      • Jonathan Edwards
        • Freedom of Will, the Doctrine of Original Sin Defended, The Nature of True Virtue
        • He wanted to restore the listeners the original sense of religious commitment which he thought had been lost. 他想让听众恢复他认为已经失去的原始宗教承诺感。
        • His sermon Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God, which was powerful in the use of logic and creative in the use of metaphor, became the most famous sermon in colonial America. 他布道愤怒的上帝手中的罪人，在使用逻辑方面很有力量，在使用比喻方面很有创意，成为美国殖民地时期最有名的布道。
        • Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God 愤怒的上帝手中的罪人们
          • "There is nothing that keeps wicked men at any one moment out of hell, but the mere pleasure of God." 唯有上帝的喜悦能免除你的地狱之灾。
          • In the sermon, Edwards terrorized his listeners using creative metaphor with visions of unregenerate men dangled over the pit of hell by an angry God. It was an exposition of God's vindictive justice and man's natural corruption. However, he intent to awaken in them a new sense of sin, and to receive God's grace. The underlying point is that God has given humanity a chance to rectify their sins. Edwards says that it is the will of God that keeps wicked men from the depths of Hell. This act of restraint has given humanity a chance to mend their ways and return to Christ. 在布道中，爱德华兹用创造性的比喻恐吓他的听众，幻想未重生的人被愤怒的上帝吊在地狱的坑里。这是对上帝的报复性正义和人类的自然腐败的阐述。然而，他的意图是唤醒他们对罪的新认识，并接受神的恩典。其基本观点是，上帝给了人类一个纠正其罪过的机会。爱德华兹说，是上帝的意志使恶人远离地狱的深处。这种约束行为给了人类一个改过自新、回到基督身边的机会。
          • Edwards adopts different rhetorical devices to frighten his audience including Analogy, Metaphor, personification, Rhetorical question. 爱德华兹采用了不同的修辞手法来吓唬他的听众，包括比喻、隐喻、拟人、反问。
    • Romanticisms (1820-1865) 浪漫主义
      • Washington Irving, James Fennimore Copper, Herman Melville: Moby Dick, Edgar Allan Poe, Henry Wadsworth Longfellow, Walt Whitman, Emily Dickinson, Nathaniel Hawthorne *(Puritanism, Romanticism, Transcendentalism)
      • 时期综述
        • American literature became intense, personal and symbolic. Romanticism emphasizes sentiment over reason. It views the individual at the very center of all life and regards literature as an expression of this thoughts and feelings. (Freedom of thought and expression) It places increasing value on spontaneity in the expression and the power of individual's imagination. Romanticists seek pure beauty and idealize nature as a retreat from industrial society. They also Seek the absolute and the ideal by transcending the actual. 美国文学变得紧张、个人化和象征化。浪漫主义强调感性而非理性。它认为个人是所有生活的中心，并将文学视为这种思想和情感的表达。(思想和表达的自由）它越来越重视表达的自发性和个人想象的力量。浪漫主义者寻求纯粹的美，并将自然理想化，作为对工业社会的回避。他们还通过超越现实来寻求绝对和理想。
        • 文学特点：Romanticism frequently shared certain universal characteristics: moral enthusiasm, faith in the value of individualism and intuitive perception, and a presumption that natural world is a source of beauty and human's society is corrupted.浪漫主义经常具有某些普遍特征：道德热情、对个人主义和直觉感知价值的信仰，以及对自然界是美的源泉和人类社会被腐蚀的假设。
      • Nathaniel Hawthorne
        • The Scarlet letter, Mosses from an Old Mansion, The House of the Seven Gables, The Marble Faun
        • Hawthorne's works belong to romanticism or, more specifically, dark romanticism, cautionary tales that suggest that guilt, sin, and evil are the most inherent natural qualities of humanity. Hawthorne had a feeling of Puritanism as being intolerant and cruel. Although he was appalled by the Puritan injustice, he was convinced that there was both good and evil in Puritanism. His stories display a psychological insight into moral isolation and human emotion. He was fond of using symbols to reveal the psychology of his characters. He used masks veils, shadows, emblems to give dramatic forms to the universal dilemmas of humanity. 霍桑的作品属于浪漫主义，或者更确切地说，属于黑暗的浪漫主义，是警示性的故事，暗示内疚、罪恶和邪恶是人类最固有的自然品质。霍桑对清教主义有一种感觉，认为它是不宽容的、残酷的。尽管他对清教的不公正感到震惊，但他相信清教主义中既有善也有恶。他的故事显示出对道德隔离和人类情感的心理洞察力。他喜欢用象征物来揭示人物的心理。他用面具、阴影、徽记来为人类的普遍困境赋予戏剧性的形式。
        • Anti-Transcendentalist, guilt & secrecy, ethical problems of sin, punishment & atonement 反超验主义，内疚与秘密，罪的伦理问题，惩罚与赎罪
        • The Minister's Black Veil 教长的黑面纱
          • The Black Veil is used as a daily reminder of people's secret sins, undeniable truths, guilt, and secrets that they are just unwilling to admit. People hide the sin consciously or unconsciously from their nearest and dearest, so everybody is wearing an invisible black veil. 黑纱被用作日常提醒人们的秘密罪孽、不可否认的事实、内疚和只是不愿承认的秘密。人们自觉或不自觉地对他们最亲近的人隐藏罪恶，所以每个人都戴着无形的黑纱。
          • The Black Veil visualize the hiding of secret sin. No mortal can see the hidden sin, but it can never escape from the eyes of God. Hawthorne's use of Hooper's veil teaches that whether we face it or not, we all sin and must accept what we have done, because judgment will come for everyone. Hooper decides to represent hidden sin and guilt in a literal way to reach out to his followers.黑纱形象地说明了隐藏秘密的罪。凡人无法看到隐藏的罪，但它永远无法逃出上帝的眼睛。霍桑用胡伯的面纱告诉人们，无论我们是否面对它，我们都有罪，必须接受自己的所作所为，因为审判会降临到每个人身上。胡伯决定以文字的方式表现隐藏的罪恶和内疚，向他的追随者传递信息。
          • Hooper pays a high price for this parable: The community's admiration for him turns to confusion and fear and awe, and he is forced to live a lonely, isolated life. The focus exclusively on Hooper's sinfulness because, deep down, they recognize their own, and don't want to acknowledge it. 胡伯为这个寓言付出了高昂的代价。社区对他的钦佩变成了迷惑、恐惧和敬畏，他被迫过着孤独、寂寞的生活。人们只关注胡伯的罪过，因为在内心深处，他们认识到自己的罪过，但却不想承认它。
          • The black veil --- a thin clothing is a symbol of the superficiality of Puritan society. The townspeople judge Hooper on his appearance, not his behavior or his character.黑纱---薄薄的衣服是清教徒社会肤浅的象征。镇民们根据胡伯的外表来判断他，而不是他的行为或他的性格。
      • Emily Dickinson
        • Wild Nights-Wild Nights!, I Died for Beauty-But Was Scarce,
        • Dickinson's poems are written in a wry voice, with sharp, unusual images and punctuation. She developed her own poetic form with many peculiar features, such as the abundant use of dashes, and irregular and often idiosyncratic punctuation and capitalization. Her mode of expression is characterized fragmentary and enigmatic metrical pattern. 迪金森的诗以诙谐的口吻写成，形象鲜明，标点符号不同寻常。她发展了自己的诗歌形式，有许多奇特的特点，如大量使用破折号，以及不规则的、往往是特异的标点符号和大写字母。她的表达方式的特点是碎片化和神秘的格律模式。
        • A Bird Came down the Walk- 鸟儿沿着小径过来
          • The poem presents a contrast between the civilized world and the natural world. Man is gradually alienated from nature.这首诗呈现了文明世界和自然世界之间的对比。人与自然逐渐疏远。
          • The bird becomes an emblem for the quick, lively, ungraspable wild essence that distances nature from human beings who desire to appropriate or tame it.这只鸟成为快速、活泼、无法把握的野性本质的象征，使自然界与渴望占有或驯服它的人类拉开距离。
          • At the end, the bird flies away represented that humanity maintains that all creature are inferior to mankind, yet it is nature itself who struggles to avoid the dominant of human being. 最后，这只鸟飞走了，代表着人类认为所有生物都不如人类，然而正是大自然本身在努力避免人类的统治。
        • Because I Could not stop for Death- 因为我不能停步等候死神
          • Death is personified as a courtly suitor and as the fraudulent seducer (gentleman). The poem reflects a basic ambiguity about death and immortality. In the last stanza, the narrator doubts the real destination is eternity but not immortality. They passed four symbolic places. 死亡被拟人化为宫廷式的求婚者和欺诈性的引诱者（绅士）。这首诗反映了关于死亡和不朽的基本模糊性。在最后一节中，叙述者怀疑真正的目的地是永恒，但不是不朽。他们经过了四个象征性的地方
          • niMetaphor
            • School ---childhood
            • The setting sun --- old age
            • Eternity vs. Immortality
            • Immortality = never die whatever happens
            • Fields of Gazing Grain --- middle age
            • A swelling of the ground ---tomb/grave
            • Eternity = can die if not sustained
      • Walt Whitman
        • Leaves of Grass, Song of Myself, Out of the Cradle Endlessly Rocking, Cavalry Crossing a Ford, Come Up from the Fields, Father (cruel war, loss of life emotional and psychological suffering)
        • Walt Whitman created free verse, which has no regular meter, rhythm or line length. He extols the ideals of democracy and equality and celebrates the dignity, and the joy of the common man. His poem reveals a world without rank and hierarchy. Though he was not a transcendentalist, he was influenced by Emerson. 沃尔特-惠特曼创造了自由诗，它没有固定的韵律、节奏或行长。他颂扬民主和平等的理想，赞美普通人的尊严和快乐。他的诗揭示了一个没有等级和层次的世界。虽然他不是超验主义者，但他受到了爱默生的影响。
        • When I Heard the Learn'd Astronomer 一堂天文课
          • Walt Whitman creates contrast in rhythmic pattern, character between the astronomer and the narrator to represent two different modes of knowing, or two different ways of looking at the universe: the scientific way and the transcendental way. He has a firm belief in the individual that he can understand the universe by looking up the sky. 沃尔特-惠特曼在天文学家和叙述者之间创造了节奏模式和性格上的对比，代表了两种不同的认识模式，或者说两种不同的观察宇宙的方式：科学方式和超验方式。他对个人有坚定的信念，认为他可以通过仰望天空来了解宇宙
    • Transcendentalism (1835-1860) 超验主义
      • Henry David Thoreau, Margaret Fuller, Ralph Waldo Emerson, Nathaniel Hawthorne* (Puritanism, Romanticism, Transcendentalism)
      • 时期综述
        • The ultimate characteristics is “the belief that man can intuitively transcend the limits of the scenes and those of logic, and receive directly higher truths and greater knowledge which is denied to the mundane methods of knowing.其最终特征是 "相信人可以凭直觉超越场景和逻辑的限制，直接接受更高的真理和更大的知识，这是被世俗的认识方法所否定的。
        • Its fundamental base: A monism holding to the unity of the world and God and the immanence of God in the world. The intuitive faculty is the means for a conscious union of the individual psyche with the world's psyche (Over soul) and God.它的基本基础。一元论认为世界和上帝的统一，以及上帝在世界中的内在性。直觉能力是个人心理与世界心理（超过灵魂）和上帝自觉结合的手段。
        • Within the nature of man there was an intuitive and personal revelation that can transcend human experience. The individual soul can reach God without the help of the church or clergy.在人的本性中，有一种直观的、个人的启示，可以超越人类的经验。个人的灵魂可以在没有教会或神职人员的帮助下达到上帝。
        • Man should rely on his own intuition and not any social laws, scientific understanding or empirical information that is given him by senses to learn the real truth. They developed the doctrine of self-reliance, individualism and non-conformity. Transcendentalism disregards external authority, tradition and logical demonstration.人应该依靠自己的直觉，而不是依靠任何社会规律、科学认识或感官给予的经验信息来了解真正的真理。他们发展了自力更生、个人主义和不循规蹈矩的学说。超验主义无视外部权威、传统和逻辑论证。
        • Source of Truth: Cosmic order 宇宙运行规律
        • Access to truth: Intuition 直觉
      • Ralph Waldo Emersion
        • Nature (integrity, intellectual freedom, self-reliance), The American Scholar, The Divinity School Address, The Transcendentalist,
        • Essays, Representing Men, English Traits, The Conduct of Life, Society and Solitude, May-Day and Other Pieces (poem)
        • He was the most eloquent spokesman of transcendentalism, advocating a direct intuition of a spiritual and immanent God in nature. He expounded his views on the mystical harmonies of man and nature, the essential perfectibility of the human spirit, the unity of the human soul with the divine Over soul, and the values of non-conformity, intellectual and spiritual independence, and self-reliance. 他是超验主义最雄辩的代言人，主张对自然界中的精神和内在的上帝有直接的直观感受。他阐述了他对人与自然的神秘和谐、人类精神的基本可完善性、人类灵魂与超灵魂性的统一性，以及不循规蹈矩、智力和精神独立和自力更生的价值观的看法。
        • Self-Reliance 自助
          • Trust thyself
          • In this essay, Emerson maintains that one must trust oneself, and never imitate others. He appeals to people not to be a conformist, and suggests that the terror that scares people from self-trust is consistency. "To be great is to be misunderstood." He believes in solitude and the community is distraction to self-growth, so he encourages people to stay at home and develop their own culture, and focusing on individual rather than societal progress. "what I must do is all that concerns me, not what the people think.” 在这篇文章中，爱默生坚持认为，人必须相信自己，而决不能模仿别人。他呼吁人们不要做一个循规蹈矩的人，并提出使人们不敢自我信任的恐怖是一致性。"成为伟大的人就是被误解的人"。他相信孤独，社会对自我成长是有干扰的，所以他鼓励人们呆在家里，发展自己的文化，关注个人而不是社会的进步。"我必须做的是我所关心的一切，而不是人们的想法。”
    • Realism (1860-1880) 现实主义
      • Bret Harte, Harriet Beecher Stowe, Kate Chopin, Mary E. W. Freeman, Charles W Chesnutt, Hamlin Garland, Edith Wharton, Mary Austin, William Bean Howells, Henry James, Mark Twain (Samuel Langhorne Clemens)
      • 时期综述
        • A literary doctrine that called for "reality and truth" in the depiction of ordinary life. Realism is "nothing more and nothing less than the truthful treatment of material." Realists reacts against romanticism and sentimentalism and portrays American life as it really is, insisting that the ordinary and the local are most suitable for artistic representation. 一种文学学说，要求在描绘普通生活时做到 "现实和真实"。现实主义是 "不外乎真实地处理材料"。现实主义者对浪漫主义和感伤主义作出反应，描绘美国生活的真实面貌，坚持认为普通和地方最适合于艺术表现。
        • The Gilded Age 镀金时代, an age of excess and extremes, of decline and progress, of poverty and dazzling wealth, of gloom and buoyant hope. 一个过度和极端的时代，一个衰退和进步的时代，一个贫穷和令人眼花缭乱的财富的时代，一个阴霾和蓬勃希望的时代。
        • 文学特点
          1. Verisimilitude (真实性) of detail derived from observation; 从观察中得到的细节的真实性
          1. A reliance on the representative in plot, setting and character; 在情节、环境和人物方面对代表人物的依赖。
          1. An objective rather than an idealized view of human nature and experience;对人性和经验的客观而非理想化的看法。
          1. Local color: an amalgam of romantic plots and realistic descriptions of the things immediately observable: the dialect, customs, sights and sounds of regional America. 地方色彩：浪漫的情节和对可立即观察到的事物的现实描述的混合体：美国地区的方言、习俗、风景和声音。
      • Mark Twain (Samuel Langhorne Clemens)
        • The Adventures of Tom Sawyer 汤姆索亚历险记, The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn口语体的 顽童历险记 (colloquial style), Life on the Mississippi 密西西比河上的生活, The Gilded Age 镀金时代
        • Mark Twain wrote a lot of novels, short stories and "tall tales", which are unique variation of American literary realism, it refers to the particular concern about the local character of a region. Mark Twain connects the frontier spirit with his fondness of local color. Mark Twain is an outstanding humorist and satirist. He has a genuine hatred for hypocrisy and pretentiousness, and expresses it in a humorous manner. 马克-吐温写了大量的小说、短篇小说和 "高大的故事"，这是美国文学现实主义的独特变体，它指的是对一个地区的地方特色的特别关注。马克-吐温将边疆精神与他对地方色彩的喜爱联系起来。马克-吐温是一位杰出的幽默家和讽刺家。他对虚伪和自命不凡抱有真诚的憎恶，并以幽默的方式表达出来。
        • The Celebrated Jumping Frog of Calaveras County 卡拉维拉斯县驰名的跳蛙
          • The story is regarded as an example of tall tale. Twain's use of humor and exaggeration is remarkable, so is the tale's satirical focus on existing cultural differences between the western and eastern regions of the US. Humor is produced by the contrast which creates irony over a simple but opportunistic Smiley as one of westerners against the "stranger" who symbolizes civilized but hypocrite easterners. The two characters in the story represent the two divergent groups in the same era of the gold rush using the typical way of entertainment in Gold rush.这个故事被认为是一个高大上的故事。吐温对幽默和夸张的运用非常出色，故事对美国东西部地区现有文化差异的讽刺也很突出。幽默是通过对比产生的，这种对比对作为西方人之一的斯迈利与象征着文明但虚伪的东方人的 "陌生人 "形成了讽刺。故事中的两个人物用淘金热中的典型娱乐方式代表了同一时代的两个不同群体。
    • Naturalism (1865-1194) 自然主义
      • Stephen Crane, Frank Norris, Theodore Dreiser. Ambrose Bierce. Edwin Arlington Robinson, O. Henry, Willa Cather, Jack London
      • 时期综述
        • Naturalism is a new and harsher realism. It develops on the basis of realism but goes a step further in portraying social reality. They present characters of low social and economic classes who were dominated by the force of environment and heredity. The naturalists emphasized a sense of determinism that human beings as passive victims of natural forces and social environment, that men and women had no free will, the religious "truths" were illusory, that the destiny of humanity was misery in life and oblivion in death. 自然主义是一种新的、更严厉的现实主义。它在现实主义的基础上发展，但在描写社会现实方面更进一步。他们表现的是社会和经济地位低下的人物，他们被环境和遗传的力量所支配。自然主义者强调一种决定论，认为人类是自然力量和社会环境的被动受害者，男人和女人没有自由意志，宗教 "真理 "是虚幻的，人类的命运是生前的苦难和死后的遗忘。
        • Darwinism 达尔文主义 : an evident influence on naturalism, stress the animality of man, to suggest that be was dominated by the irresistible forces of evolution. Darwin's theory of evolution- the theories of survival by social selection" and "survival of the fittest".对自然主义有明显的影响，强调人的灵性，暗示被不可抗拒的进化力量所支配。达尔文的进化论--社会选择的生存 "和 "适者生存 "的理论。
        • 文学特点
          • Man is taken as an animal in the natural world, responding to the environmental forces and internal stresses and drives, over none of which has control and none of which he fully understands. 人被视为自然界中的动物，对环境力量和内部压力和驱动力作出反应，对这些力量和驱动力都没有控制，也没有完全理解。
          • Subjects in Naturalist fictions:
            1. Hunger or starvation 饥饿
            1. Sexual obsession 性瘾
            1. Hereditary defects 遗传性缺陷

Thus, Holden's alienation is the source of what little stability he has in his life. He depends upon his alienation, but it destroys him. 在整部小说中，霍尔登似乎被他周围的世界所排斥，并成为受害者。随着小说的进展，我们开始意识到霍尔登的疏远是他保护自己的方式。就像他戴着猎帽（见下文 "象征物"）来宣传自己的独特性一样，他用自己的孤立来证明自己比周围的人都要好，因此不能与他们交往。事实是，与其他人的互动通常会让他感到困惑和不知所措，而他那玩世不恭的优越感则是一种自我保护的方式。因此，霍尔登的疏离感是他生活中仅有的一点稳定性的来源。他依赖他的疏离感，但疏离感却毁了他。
            • THE PAINFULNESS OF GROWING UP 成长的痛苦
              • The Catcher in the Rye is a bildungsroman, a novel about a young character's growth into maturity. But Holden's central goal is to resist the process of maturity itself. As his thoughts about the Museum of Natural History demonstrate, Holden fears change and is overwhelmed by complexity. He wants everything to be easily understandable and eternally fixed.麦田里的守望者 是一部成长小说，是一部关于一个年轻人物成长为成熟的小说。但霍尔登的中心目标是抵制成熟的过程本身。正如他对自然历史博物馆的想法所表明的那样，霍尔登害怕变化，被复杂的事物所淹没。他希望一切都容易理解并永远固定下来。
              • Holden invents a fantasy that adulthood is a world of superficiality and hypocrisy ("phoniness"), while childhood is a world of innocence, curiosity, and honesty. He imagines childhood as an idyllic field of rye in which children romp and play; adulthood, for the children of this world, is equivalent to death--a fatal fall over the edge of a cliff. 霍尔登编造了一个幻想，认为成年是一个肤浅和虚伪的世界（"虚假"），而童年是一个纯真、好奇和诚实的世界。他把童年想象成一片田园牧歌式的黑麦田，孩子们在其中嬉戏玩耍；成年后，对这个世界的孩子来说，就相当于死亡--从悬崖边上摔下来的致命一击。
            • THE PHONINESS OF THE ADULT WORLD 成人世界的虚假性
              • "Phoniness," which is probably the most famous phrase from The Catcher in the Rye, is one of Holden's favorite concepts. It is his catch-all for describing the superficiality, hypocrisy, pretension, and shallowness that he encounters in the world around him. In Chapter 22, just before he reveals his fantasy of the catcher in the rye, Holden explains that adults are inevitably phonies, and, what's worse, they can't see their own phoniness. Phoniness, for Holden, stands as an emblem of everything that's wrong in the world around him and provides an excuse for him to withdraw into his cynical isolation. "虚假 "可能是《麦田里的守望者》中最著名的短语，是霍尔登最喜欢的概念之一。这是他描述他在周围世界中遇到的肤浅、虚伪、装腔作势和浅薄的总称。在第22章中，就在他揭示他对麦田里的捕手的幻想之前，霍尔顿解释说，成年人不可避免地是假的，而且更糟糕的是，他们看不到自己的假。对霍尔登来说，虚假是他周围世界一切问题的象征，并为他提供了一个借口，让他退回到愤世嫉俗的孤立状态。
          • Holden
            • By making such an unorthodox and unreliable character as Holden the narrator, Salinger subtly suggest the reader must always read between the lines like a detective looking for hints and clues that might help explain which of Holden's insight are valid and which are as phony as the phoniness he condemns. Taking Holden as the narrator, a voice seemingly from real life is more authentic than an author telling us the story. The teenage idioms indicate a strong mood of psychological suffering. Holden's major problem is his overwhelmingly negative attitude toward everything and everyone around him and confronts him on this fault. His appreciation of the suicide of James Castle his believe James maintain his innocence. 塞林格让霍尔登这样一个非正统的、不可靠的人物作为叙述者，巧妙地暗示读者必须始终像侦探一样在字里行间寻找暗示和线索，以帮助解释霍尔登的哪些见解是正确的，哪些是和他谴责的虚假一样的假象。以霍尔登为叙述者，一个似乎来自真实生活的声音比一个作者给我们讲故事更真实。青少年的成语表明了一种强烈的心理痛苦情绪。霍尔登的主要问题是他对周围的一切事物和人都抱有压倒性的消极态度，并直面他的这个过失。他对詹姆士的自杀表示赞赏，他认为詹姆士保持了他的清白。
          • Motif
            • LONELINESS 孤独
              • Loneliness is the emotional manifestation of the alienation Holden experiences; it is both a source of great pain and a source of his security. 孤独是霍尔登所经历的异化的情感表现；它既是巨大痛苦的来源，也是他安全感的来源。
            • RELATIONSHIPS, INTIMACY, AND SEXUALITY 关系、亲密关系和性行为
              • Relationships, intimacy, and sexuality are also recurring motifs relating to the larger theme of alienation. Both physical and emotional relationships offer Holden opportunity to break out of his isolated shell. They also represent what he fears most about the adult world: complexity, unpredictability, and potential for conflict and change. As a result, he has isolated himself and fears intimacy. Although he encounters opportunities for both physical and emotional intimacy, he bungles them all, wrapping himself in a psychological armor of critical cynicism and bitterness. Even so, Holden desperately continues searching for new relationships, always undoing himself only at the last moment. 关系、亲密关系和性行为也是与疏离这个大主题有关的反复出现的主题。身体和情感关系都为霍尔登提供了打破其孤立的外壳的机会。它们也代表了他对成人世界最害怕的东西：复杂性、不可预测性以及冲突和变化的可能性。因此，他把自己孤立起来，害怕亲密关系。尽管他遇到了身体和情感上的亲密接触的机会，但他把这些机会都搞砸了，把自己包裹在批判性嘲讽和痛苦的心理盔甲里。即便如此，霍尔登还是拼命地继续寻找新的关系，但总是在最后一刻才把自己弄垮。
            • LYING AND DECEPTION 撒谎和欺骗
              • Lying and deception are the most obvious and hurtful elements of the larger category of phoniness. Of course, Holden himself is guilty of both these crimes. His random and repeated lying highlights his own self-deception-he refuses to acknowledge his own shortcomings. Through his lying and deception, Holden proves that he is just as guilty of phoniness as the people he criticizes. 撒谎和欺骗是 "虚伪 "这个大类别中最明显、最伤人的元素。当然，霍尔登自己也犯有这两种罪行。他随意和反复的撒谎突出了他自己的自欺欺人，他拒绝承认自己的缺点。通过他的谎言和欺骗，霍尔登证明了他和他所批评的人一样犯有虚假的罪行。
          • Symbols
            • THE "CATCHER IN THE RYE"
              • When Phoebe asks Holden what he wants to do with his life, he replies with his image, from the song, of a "catcher in the rye." Holden imagines a field of rye perched high on a cliff, full of children romping and playing. He says he would like to protect the children from falling off the edge of the cliff by "catching" them if they were on the verge of tumbling over. Holden wants to catch children before they fall out of innocence into knowledge of the adult world, including knowledge of sex. 当菲比问霍尔登他想用自己的生命做什么时，他用歌曲中的 "麦田里的捕手 "的形象作了回答。霍尔登想象着一块高悬在悬崖上的黑麦田，里面有很多孩子在嬉戏玩耍。他说他想保护孩子们不从悬崖边上掉下来，如果他们快要翻倒的话，他就 "抓住 "他们。霍尔登想在孩子们从天真无邪跌入成人世界的知识，包括性知识之前抓住他们。
            • HOLDEN'S RED HUNTING HAT
              • It is inseparable from our image of Holden, with good reason: it is a symbol of his uniqueness and individuality. The hat is outlandish, and it shows that Holden desires to be different from everyone around him. At the same time, he is very self-conscious about the hat--he always mentions when he is wearing it, and he often doesn't wear it if he is going to be around people he knows. The presence of the hat, therefore, mirrors the central conflict in the book: Holden's need for isolation versus his need for companionship. 它与我们对霍尔登的印象密不可分，这是有道理的：它是他的独特性和个性的象征。这顶帽子很离奇，它表明霍尔登渴望与周围的人不同。同时，他对这顶帽子很有自知之明--他总是提到他什么时候戴着这顶帽子，而且如果他要和他认识的人在一起，他常常不戴。因此，这顶帽子的存在反映了书中的核心冲突。霍尔登对孤独的需求和对同伴的需求。
            • THE MUSEUM OF NATURAL HISTORY
              • Holden tells us the symbolic meaning of the museum's displays: they appeal to him because they are frozen and unchanging. He also mentions that he is troubled by the fact that he has changed every time he returns to them. The museum represents the world Holden wishes he could live in: a world where nothing ever changes, where everything is simple, understandable, and infinite. Holden is terrified by the unpredictable challenges of the world--he hates conflict, he is confused by Allie's senseless death, and he fears interaction with other people. 霍尔登告诉我们博物馆陈列品的象征意义：它们吸引他是因为它们是凝固的、不变的。他还提到，他对自己每次回到这些展品时都有变化这一事实感到不安。博物馆代表了霍尔登希望自己能生活在其中的世界：一个没有任何变化的世界，那里的一切都很简单，可以理解，而且是无限的。霍尔登被这个世界不可预测的挑战吓坏了--他讨厌冲突，他对艾莉无意义的死亡感到困惑，他害怕与其他人交往。
            • THE DUCKS IN THE CENTRAL PARK LAGOON
              • Holden's curiosity about where the ducks go during the winter reveals a genuine, more youthful side to his character. For most of the book, he sounds like a grumpy old man who is angry at the world, but his search for the ducks represents the curiosity of youth and a joyful willingness to encounter the mysteries of the world. It is a memorable moment, because Holden clearly lacks such willingness in other aspects of his life.霍尔登对鸭子在冬季去向的好奇心揭示了他性格中真实、更年轻的一面。在这本书的大部分时间里，他听起来像是一个对世界感到愤怒的脾气暴躁的老人，但他对鸭子的寻找却代表了年轻人的好奇心和对世界之谜的快乐意愿。这是一个令人难忘的时刻，因为霍尔登在他生活的其他方面显然缺乏这种意愿。
              • The ducks and their pond are symbolic in several ways. Their mysterious perseverance in the face of an inhospitable environment resonates with Holden's understanding of his own situation. In addition, the ducks prove that some vanishings are only temporary. Traumatized and made acutely aware of the fragility of life by his brother Allie's death, Holden is terrified by the idea of change and disappearance. The ducks vanish every winter, but they return every spring, thus symbolizing change that isn't permanent, but cyclical. Finally, the pond itself becomes a minor metaphor for the world as Holden sees it, because it is "partly frozen and partly not frozen." The pond is in transition between two states, just as Holden is in transition between childhood and adulthood. 鸭子和它们的池塘在几个方面具有象征意义。它们面对荒凉环境的神秘毅力，与霍尔登对自身处境的理解产生了共鸣。此外，鸭子证明了有些消失只是暂时的。霍尔登因弟弟艾莉的死而受到创伤，并敏锐地意识到生命的脆弱，他对变化和消失的想法感到恐惧。鸭子每年冬天都会消失，但它们每年春天都会回来，因此象征着变化不是永久的，而是周期性的。最后，池塘本身成为霍尔登眼中世界的一个小隐喻，因为它 "一部分是冰冻的，一部分是不冻的"。池塘处于两种状态之间的过渡，正如霍尔登处于童年和成年之间的过渡。
